Requirements for Acquiring a Cryptocurrency License in Hong Kong

Until recently, the government authorities in Hong Kong maintained a neutral stance regarding the licensing of cryptocurrency exchanges within the country. However, with the recent amendments to the Anti-Money Laundering and Counter-Terrorist Financing Act, enacted in December 2022, new regulations governing cryptocurrency activities in Hong Kong came into effect on April 1, 2023. As per these regulations, all virtual asset service providers (VASPs) are obligated to obtain a cryptocurrency license from the Securities and Futures Commission (SFC) to engage in legal cryptocurrency activities.

Individuals or companies lacking this license are prohibited from:

  • Conducting a business that offers any virtual asset services (VA).
  • Representing themselves as businesses providing VA services.
  • Performing regulated functions for businesses providing VA services.
  • Presenting themselves as individuals performing such regulated functions.
  • Advertising VA services.

The Hong Kong Securities and Futures Commission (SFC) commenced the acceptance of applications for cryptocurrency trading platform licenses on June 1, 2023.

As outlined in the decree, VA services, when conducted electronically, include:

  1. Regularly making offers to sell or buy virtual assets resulting in a binding transaction.
  2. Regularly introducing or identifying persons to others to negotiate or transact sales or purchases of virtual assets resulting in a binding transaction.
  3. Handling client money or client virtual assets that come into the direct or indirect possession of the service provider.

Cryptocurrency trading platforms facilitating the trading of financial products (securities and futures contracts) are exempt from the new licensing system, as they are already regulated under the Securities and Futures Ordinance (SFO).

It’s crucial to emphasize that the new licensing system is applicable to all cryptocurrency exchanges registered in Hong Kong under the Companies Ordinance (Chapter 622). This includes exchanges operating outside of Hong Kong but actively attracting Hong Kong citizens.

To secure a license in Hong Kong, the applicant is required to:

1.Clearly express the intention to offer services in the Hong Kong market.

2.Successfully pass a fit and proper test, which involves verifying:

  • The absence of any criminal record.
  • The history of anti-money laundering/counter-terrorist financing (AML/CFT) activities.
  • Financial situation.
  • Educational background or other qualifications.
  • The individual’s reputation, experience, character, reliability, and financial integrity.

3.Specify in the application at least two individuals considered suitable and qualified to undertake virtual asset (VA) services, applying for the position of responsible officers.

4.Apply for approval of the premises designated for the storage of records or documents mandated under the Regulation.

The Securities and Futures Commission (SFC) retains the authority to impose conditions on the granted license, encompassing aspects such as risk management, AML/CFT measures, financial resources, cybersecurity, and others, as outlined in Section 53ZRK of the Regulation.

The application must be submitted to the Commission following established procedures and accompanied by the requisite fees.

Reasons for choosing Hong Kong

Hong Kong offers a number of advantages for cryptocurrency companies, including a stable legal system, an attractive tax policy with a low corporate income tax rate and no capital gains tax, and a high degree of financial innovation and technological readiness.

Before starting the registration process, it is necessary to carefully plan the business model and structure of the company, taking into account the specifics of the cryptocurrency market and the regulatory requirements of Hong Kong. Choosing a name for your company is the first step in the process. You need to make sure that the name you choose is not registered by another person. To do this, you can use the online company name checker provided by the Hong Kong Registration Chamber.

Step 3: Company registration

Registering a company in Hong Kong involves submitting the necessary documents to Companies Registry. The required documents include:

  • Statutory Declaration;
  • Company Statute;
  • Documents confirming the appointment of directors and company secretary;
  • Confirmation of registered address in Hong Kong.

Step 4: Opening a bank account

Opening a corporate bank account is a key step to running a business. In Hong Kong, many banks have certain requirements for companies dealing with cryptocurrencies, so it is important to familiarise yourself with their policies in advance and provide all the necessary documentation.

Companies engaged in certain cryptocurrency activities may be subject to the regulatory requirements of the Securities and Futures Commission of Hong Kong (SFC).  Crypto legislation in Hong Kong

Cryptocurrency legislation in Hong Kong is characterised by its progressive nature and aims to create a favourable environment for the development of innovative financial technologies while ensuring investor protection and financial market integrity. Hong Kong endeavours to balance innovation and regulation by offering a clear and flexible regulatory framework for cryptocurrency companies and their customers. In this article, we look at the key aspects of cryptocurrency legislation in Hong Kong that all market participants should be aware of.

Regulator frame

The main regulatory body responsible for overseeing cryptocurrency transactions in Hong Kong is the Securities and Futures Commission of Hong Kong (SFC). The SFC plays a key role in shaping and enforcing regulations relating to cryptocurrencies and related financial products and services.

Licensing and regulation

In November 2018, the SFC introduced a new licensing system for fund managers that invest in cryptocurrencies as well as digital asset trading platforms. This means that all cryptocurrency trading platforms wishing to operate in Hong Kong must be licensed by the SFC and comply with its risk management, customer protection and anti-money laundering requirements.

Investor protection

Protecting the rights and interests of investors is central to Hong Kong’s regulatory policy on cryptocurrencies. Companies receiving an SFC licence are required to comply with strict disclosure requirements, ensure fair and transparent trading and prevent conflicts of interest.

Anti-Money Laundering (AML) and Know Your Customer (KYC)

Cryptocurrency companies in Hong Kong are required to implement effective anti-money laundering and counter-terrorist financing systems, as well as customer identification and verification procedures. This includes requirements for record keeping, transaction monitoring and suspicious transaction reporting.

Policy on ICOs

The SFC has also published guidelines for initial coin offerings (ICOs), emphasising that many tokens issued during an ICO may be considered securities. This means that ICO organisers must comply with the SFC’s regulatory requirements if their tokens qualify as securities. This includes licensing and registration obligations, as well as fulfilling all relevant investor protection obligations. For ICOs whose tokens are not treated as securities, the general rules and regulations still apply, especially those relating to anti-money laundering and counter-terrorist financing.

STO regulation

In addition, Hong Kong is actively exploring and adapting to new forms of financing, including tokenised securities offerings (STOs). Similar to ICOs, STOs in Hong Kong must comply with SFC regulatory requirements if they offer tokens that can be classified as securities. This emphasises Hong Kong’s intention to support innovation in the financial sector while ensuring a high level of investor protection and market stability.

Regulatory sandboxes and innovative programmes

To encourage innovation and testing of new technologies in a controlled environment, the SFC and other Hong Kong regulators have developed the concept of regulatory sandboxes. These platforms allow cryptocurrency startups and fintech companies to test their products and services under certain conditions before a full market launch.

Future changes and adaptation to international standards

Hong Kong continues to actively monitor global trends and international standards in cryptocurrency regulation. To enhance its reputation as one of the world’s leading financial centres, Hong Kong is adapting its regulatory framework to comply with the recommendations of the Financial Action Task Force (FATF) and other international organisations.

Conclusion

Cryptocurrency legislation in Hong Kong is a comprehensive and multi-layered approach to the regulation of digital financial assets that seeks to protect investors and support innovation. Understanding and complying with these regulatory requirements is key to the successful operation of cryptocurrency companies in Hong Kong. It is important to note that the regulatory environment is constantly evolving and companies need to stay up-to-date with the latest developments to ensure their long-term compliance and success in the market.

In anticipation of innovation and regulatory updates, companies must proactively engage with regulators, professional advisors and legal experts to adapt to the changes and use them to their advantage. The key to success in this dynamic environment is flexibility and a willingness to continuously learn and improve.

Recommendations for companies

  1. Regular knowledge updates: Given the rapid pace of change in the regulatory environment, companies must continually update their knowledge of current and upcoming regulatory requirements.
  2. Implement strict KYC/AML procedures: Customer Knowledge (KYC) and Anti-Money Laundering (AML) compliance remains critical for all cryptocurrency transactions.
  3. Active engagement with regulators: Transparent and constructive engagement with regulators can facilitate a better understanding of requirements and help develop appropriate business models.
  4. Engaging qualified legal and financial advisors: The complexity of the regulatory environment makes it essential to have experienced advisors who can provide specialised knowledge and support.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Is cryptocurrency regulated in Hong Kong?

Certainly, in Hong Kong, cryptocurrency falls under the regulation of the Anti-Money Laundering and Counter-Terrorist Financing Act, as amended in 2022. The Securities and Futures Ordinance (SFO) and other regulations oversee securities, futures contracts, and stored value objects.

What are the tax implications for cryptocurrency in Hong Kong?

In Hong Kong, bitcoin and other decentralized cryptocurrencies are categorized as "virtual commodities" and are not recognized as legal tender. Consequently, investors in Hong Kong engaged in buying and selling cryptocurrencies are not obligated to pay capital gains tax.

However, if crypto-assets are traded as part of the "ordinary course of business," they are treated as income and subject to income tax. Individuals are subject to income tax at a rate of up to 16.5%, and legal entities are subject to income tax at a rate of 15%. Notably, Hong Kong adheres to the territorial principle of taxation, meaning income tax is applicable only if the business is conducted in Hong Kong, and profits are derived from activities within Hong Kong. Income generated outside of Hong Kong is not taxable.

Concerning taxes related to Initial Coin Offerings (ICOs), the taxation treatment depends on whether the ICO is considered an offering of securities (e.g., providing investors with voting rights) or as a futures or contract for services/goods that offer a future benefit to purchasers.

Is cryptocurrency trading permitted in Hong Kong?

Absolutely, cryptocurrency trading is allowed in Hong Kong. There are cryptocurrency exchanges and platforms that enable users to buy, sell, and exchange various cryptocurrencies.

What sanctions might a Hong Kong cryptocurrency business encounter?

Significant fines and criminal penalties await those violating cryptocurrency licensing requirements in Hong Kong. Operating without a license can result in fines of up to HK$5,000,000 ($640,000), and company management could face up to seven years in prison.

Failure to comply with established anti-money laundering and counter-terrorist financing (AML/CTF) requirements by a licensed Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) and its responsible employees could lead to a fine of HK$1,000,000 (US$128,000) and a two-year prison term if convicted on indictment.
