What types of companies exist in Liechtenstein?

Liechtenstein, despite its modest size, is an attractive jurisdiction for international business due to its economic stability, favorable tax system and flexible corporate legislation. The country offers a variety of forms of companies, each of which has its own characteristics and advantages. In this article, we will look at the key types of companies available for registration in Liechtenstein.

  1. Joint Stock Company (Aktiengesellschaft, AG)

A joint-stock company (AG) is one of the most popular forms of business in Liechtenstein, suitable for large projects and international activities. The minimum authorized capital is 50,000 Swiss francs, of which 100% must be paid at registration. AG provides shareholders with limited liability to the extent of their contribution to the share capital.

  1. Limited Liability Company (Gesellschaft mit beschrbeschrgnkter Haftung, GmbH)

GmbH is the preferred choice for small and medium-sized enterprises that require a smaller share capital compared to AG. The minimum authorized capital is 30,000 Swiss francs. The liability of participants is limited to their share of investments in the authorized capital. A GmbH is required to have at least one manager, who must be a resident of Liechtenstein.

  1. Partnership (Limited company, CO and Collective company, KG)

A partnership in Liechtenstein can be arranged either as a limited partnership (CO) or as a collective partnership (CG). These forms of businesses are ideal for family businesses or small partner projects. In a limited partnership, one or more partners are fully responsible, while others have limited liability. A collective society requires all partners to be jointly and fully responsible for the company’s obligations.

  1. Trust (Treuh&nderschaft)

Liechtenstein is known for its trust structures that offer a high level of privacy and asset protection. A Liechtenstein trust allows the founder to transfer assets to a trustee, who then manages those assets for the benefit of the beneficiaries. Trusts are widely used for inheritance planning and asset protection.

  1. Anstalt

Anstalt is a unique form of company in Liechtenstein, which can be used for commercial activities or private asset management. This is a legal entity with elements of both a joint-stock company and a foundation. Anstalt may issue shares if this is provided for in its articles of association, but it may also be owned by one person. This form is popular among international investors for asset management and investment.

  1. Foundation (Stiftung)

Foundations in Liechtenstein are usually established for long-term asset management, inheritance planning, and charitable purposes. Funds can enjoy tax benefits, depending on their structure and purpose. The minimum authorized capital depends on the purpose of the fund and can start from 30,000 Swiss francs.

Conclusion

Liechtenstein offers a wide range of corporate structures that meet the needs of international investors and local entrepreneurs. Choosing the right form of company depends on a variety of factors, including business volume, tax considerations, privacy requirements, and asset management. Due to the complexity of legislation and tax regimes, it is recommended to consult with professionals to select the optimal corporate structure in Liechtenstein.

 What is the size of the company’s authorized capital in Liechtenstein?

Liechtenstein, known for its attractive business environment, flexible corporate structures and favorable tax treatment, offers various forms of legal entities for doing business. The size of the authorized capital is one of the key aspects when registering a company. In this article, we will look at the minimum authorized capital requirements for various types of companies in Liechtenstein.

Joint Stock Company (AG)

A joint-stock company (Aktiengesellschaft, AG) in Liechtenstein has a minimum authorized capital of 50,000 Swiss francs (CHF). This capital must be paid in full at the time of incorporation of the company. AG allows its shareholders to limit their liability to the size of their contributions to the company’s capital.

Limited Liability Company (GmbH)

For a limited liability company (Gesellschaft mit beschränkter Haftung, GmbH), the minimum authorized capital is CHF 30,000. In contrast to an AG, where the capital is divided into shares, the members of a GmbH invest their shares in the company, which also limits their liability to the amount of their investment.

Limited Company (Kommanditgesellschaft, KG)

A limited company in Liechtenstein does not have a strictly defined minimum authorized capital. KG has at least one limited liability investor whose liability is limited to their contribution to the company’s capital, and one full partner who has unlimited liability.

Collective company (Kollektivgesellschaft, KG)

There is also no minimum amount of authorized capital for a collective company. All partners in a KG are jointly and unlimitedly liable for the company’s obligations, which makes this form of company less popular among investors seeking to limit their liability.

Anstalt

Anstalt is a corporate form unique to Liechtenstein that can function as a commercial organization or a holding company for asset management. The minimum authorized capital for an anstalt is set individually, depending on the structure and purpose of the company, but is often CHF 30,000.

Foundation (Stiftung)

For a foundation (Stiftung), the minimum authorized capital can also vary, but is usually CHF 30,000. Foundations are often used for asset management, inheritance and charitable purposes, offering tax benefits and a high level of confidentiality.

Conclusion

In Liechtenstein, there are a variety of forms of companies, each of which has its own requirements for the authorized capital. The choice of the appropriate form depends on a variety of factors, including the scope of activities, strategic goals, and the desired level of responsibility and confidentiality. Given the complexity and importance of these decisions, potential investors are encouraged to seek advice from qualified lawyers and financial consultants in Liechtenstein.

For a joint-stock company (AG) in Liechtenstein, the State registration fee is determined in proportion to the authorized capital. For companies with large authorized capital, the fee may be significantly higher.

Limited Liability Company (GmbH)

For a limited liability company (GmbH), state fees also depend on the authorized capital, but in general they are lower than for AG. The minimum fee for setting up a GmbH is usually around 600-800 CHF.

Partnerships (KG and KG)

For limited and collective partnerships (KG and CG), government fees are usually lower, as these forms of companies are less complex to establish and manage. Fees may vary from 300 to 500 CHF.

Anstalt and the Foundation (Stiftung)

The establishment of an anstalt or foundation (Stiftung) in Liechtenstein is accompanied by the payment of fees, the amount of which may vary significantly depending on the specifics and goals of the organization being established. On average, the fee can range from 1,000 to 2,000 CHF.

Additional Fees

In addition to the basic state fees, additional fees must also be taken into account, such as notary fees, registration fees in the commercial register and legal services costs. These fees can significantly increase the total cost of setting up a company.
